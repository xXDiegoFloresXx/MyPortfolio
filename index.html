FIRST Robotics Competition 2019

ABB Welding Robot Cell

Collaborated on an engineering project with ABB to design and develop an automated welding cell that utilized laser welding technology to join two steel components. My responsibilities included creating the detailed CAD design of the cell and conducting a comprehensive simulation using ABB Studio to validate the system’s performance. Throughout the project, I worked closely with engineers and safety specialists to ensure the cell met stringent operational time requirements while adhering to rigorous safety standards. This experience deepened my understanding of precision laser welding processes, safety considerations in automated systems, and how to integrate customer requirements into efficient, reliable manufacturing solutions.

Parker Press Cell

Collaborated with Parker on an advanced manufacturing cell project aimed at automating the production process for air conditioning valves. The primary objective was to minimize human intervention by integrating robotic arms into key stages of the assembly and machining operations. Throughout this project, I was deeply involved in analyzing and optimizing every step of the process, from material handling and component positioning to precision assembly and quality checks ensuring seamless coordination between robotic systems and existing manufacturing infrastructure. This experience greatly expanded my knowledge of industrial automation, robotic integration, and process control, while also highlighting the critical importance of safety protocols and ergonomic considerations in automated environments. Additionally, it strengthened my skills in interdisciplinary teamwork, as I collaborated closely with engineers, technicians, and operators to deliver a reliable, efficient, and scalable solution aligned with Parker’s production goals.

PID Autonomous Car

Collaborated with John Deere on an autonomous vehicle project designed to simulate the behavior of a real tractor. The core objective was to develop a small-scale robotic car capable of navigating a precise 10 x 10 meter square path with minimal deviation. To achieve this, we integrated multiple sensors including: ultrasonic, odometer devices and a camera to have a real-time obstacle detection and position tracking. We also implemented PID controllers to fine-tune the steering and speed for accurate path following. Throughout this project, I worked closely with teamates to design, program, and calibrate the control system, ensuring the autonomous car could replicate realistic tractor movements under varying conditions. This experience significantly deepened my understanding of feedback control systems, sensor fusion, and the practical challenges of autonomous navigation, while also enhancing my skills in collaborative problem solving and iterative testing to meet performance targets.

Robotic Goalkeeper

Worked on the design and development of a robotic “goalkeeper” for a tabletop game, combining mechanical design, electronics, and programming to create an interactive, automated system. The structure was primarily built using 3D-printed components and V-slot profiles, with additional elements fabricated from MDF cut according to precise CAD models we developed for optimal fit and performance. A stepper motor was integrated to control the goalkeeper’s quick, precise side-to-side movements, simulating real-time goalkeeping actions. I was actively involved in the entire process—from designing and modeling the parts in CAD software, to assembling the mechanical components, wiring the electronics, and programming the control system to ensure responsive and reliable operation. This project greatly enhanced my skills in mechatronics, precision design, and system integration, while also emphasizing the importance of iterative prototyping and effective teamwork to bring a complex interactive mechanism to life.
